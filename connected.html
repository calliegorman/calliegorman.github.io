connected

Connected is a cloud-based storage application that offers users an intuitive interface to store data, dashboard personalization and strong connection to collaborators.

The Challenge

How could I design a cloud storage solution that would be a compelling alternative to popular competitors such as Google Drive and DropBox?


The Solution

An easy to use desktop and mobile application for people wanting to save and collaborate on content. The application features an intuitive and visually appealing interface, a series of collaboration options, and dashboard personalization.

UX RESEARCH

Competitive Analysis

The cloud storage market is filled with top notch competition: Google Drive, DropBox, Box, and more. The challenge was to create a new tool that could stand up to the competition. There were four potential opportunities for connected based off of where the competition seemingly fell flat:

  1. Produce a different and more inspiring design for connected’s dashboard.
  2. Focus on branding toward the individual user vs businesses.
  3. Provide users with different ways to acquire more storage space other than paying for it.
  4. Create a personality that exudes a tone of voice that is friendly and has a sense of humor.

User Research

I conducted user research to find out what users really needed and wanted to work their best. Testing early on in the process and ensuring a subset of the respondents were users of the competition allowed me to position connected in a way that could directly resolve major pain points.

Overall, there were no clear trends in the data that suggested users were entirely unhappy with their current tools.

My challenge was to then focus on important points respondents made in the survey, and build a product that met those needs in addition to what the market was lacking.

BRANDING & VISUAL DESIGN

Mind Mapping & List Making

Two of the most important items for connected’s brand identity was personality and simplicity. The company was meant to be approachable and have a casual, fun tone of voice. The point of portraying these characteristics was to jump on the opportunity in the market that the competitors had missed - to focus on the user’s individuality.

I iterated on these ideas through mind mapping and listmaking. Soon after, the headline for connected came together in my mind to cover all users needs:

connected
to others, your unique way of working,
and the world of data around you.


Mind mapping and list making

Logo Development

While developing the logo, I thought about startup culture, and about the casual nature of using all lowercase letters and abbreviations. After several sketches, I settled on a box-like look featuring 4 letters “c” “n” “t” “d” for connected.

There were several more iterations once I got into Sketch.

It was crucial during this period to test users to gather feedback on both the user experience of the site but also the design.

Usability Testing

I conducted three usability tests after completing my prototype to ensure that the site was easy to navigate. I asked users to:

  • Sign up for an account on the homepage
  • Log into the account from the homepage
  • Add a new document in the dashboard
  • Share a file with someone
  • Tell me what they thought all of the icons in the dashboard represented

Two issues needed to be addressed in the reesign:

  • The login dropdown process was the only one that differentiated from the usual modal pop up and confused users.

  • There were sometimes too many click throughs on actions taken in the dashboard to get to the desired outcome. I took these two items into consideration and made updates.

Preference Testing

There were a few other items specifically related to visual design that I was compelled to update after preference testing:

  • Decreasing the size of icons in the dashboard
  • Ensuring most of the background image on the dashboard screen was hidden in order to make content stand out
  • Updating the section headers on the landing page from dark gray to blue

CONCLUSION

What I learned

In the end, connected succeeded in being an intuitive cloud storage product with unique branding personality. Users were able to easily navigate the website during late user testing, and appreciated the personalization of the dashboard.

This process forced me to grow as a designer. User testing was crucial, and watching how users navigated the site proved invaluable and compelled me to make several changes to the user experience and also visual design of the site.

Constant iteration and continuous feedback is a valuable asset to have. If given more time, I would have gathered an even larger user pool for surveys and testing in order to push this minimum viable product closer to a full working prototype.
